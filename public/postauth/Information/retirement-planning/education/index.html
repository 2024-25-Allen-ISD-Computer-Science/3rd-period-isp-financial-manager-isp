Core Retirement Concepts

Compound Interest

$10,000 invested at age 25 becomes $149,745 at age 65 (7% return)

  • Early investing maximizes growth
  • Returns earn additional returns
  • Time is your biggest advantage

The 4% Rule

Safe withdrawal strategy for 30-year retirement

  • Withdraw 4% first year
  • Adjust for inflation annually
  • $1M saves = $40K first year

Income Sources

Multiple streams for security

  • Social Security: $1,827/month avg
  • Personal savings & investments
  • Employer pensions if available

Retirement Account Types

Traditional 401(k)

  • 2024 Contribution Limit: $23,000
  • Catch-up Contribution (50+): Additional $7,500
  • Employer match: Typically 3-6% of salary
  • Tax-deferred growth
  • Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) start at 73
  • 10% early withdrawal penalty before 59½

Traditional IRA

  • 2024 Contribution Limit: $7,000
  • Catch-up Contribution (50+): Additional $1,000
  • Tax-deductible contributions (income limits apply)
  • Tax-deferred growth
  • RMDs start at 73
  • 10% early withdrawal penalty before 59½

Roth IRA

  • 2024 Contribution Limit: $7,000
  • Catch-up Contribution (50+): Additional $1,000
  • Income limits: Phase-out starts at $146,000 (single)
  • Tax-free growth and qualified withdrawals
  • No RMDs required
  • Contributions can be withdrawn penalty-free

SEP IRA

  • 2024 Contribution Limit: Lesser of $69,000 or 25% of compensation
  • Ideal for self-employed individuals
  • Employer contributions only
  • Tax-deductible contributions
  • RMDs start at 73
  • Simple administration

Investment Allocation Guidelines

A common rule of thumb for asset allocation:

  • Subtract your age from 110 to get your stock percentage
  • Example: At age 40, consider 70% stocks (110-40) and 30% bonds
  • Adjust based on your risk tolerance and goals

Social Security Strategy

Early Filing (Age 62)

  • Reduced benefits (up to 30% less)
  • Makes sense if you need income early
  • Health concerns or shorter life expectancy

Delayed Filing (Age 70)

  • Increased benefits (up to 32% more)
  • Good health and family longevity
  • Can afford to wait
